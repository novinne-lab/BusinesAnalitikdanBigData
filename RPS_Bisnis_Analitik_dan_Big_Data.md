# Syllabus for Business Analytics and Big Data Course

## Course Overview
This course introduces students to the essential concepts and techniques of Business Analytics and Big Data, with a focus on applying these tools in the context of digital business.

## Learning Outcomes
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
1. Understand the principles of business analytics and the role of big data in decision-making.
2. Utilize various analytics tools and techniques to derive insights from data.
3. Apply statistical methods to analyze and interpret data effectively.
4. Leverage big data technologies to solve real-world business problems.
5. Communicate analytical findings in a clear and effective manner.

## Course Content
### Week 1: Introduction to Business Analytics
- Definition and scope of business analytics
- Importance in digital business

### Week 2: Data Types and Data Sources
- Understanding structured and unstructured data
- Data collection methods

### Week 3: Statistical Foundations
- Descriptive statistics
- Inferential statistics

### Week 4: Data Visualization
- Principles of effective visualization
- Tools for data visualization

### Week 5: Predictive Analytics
- Introduction to predictive modeling
- Techniques such as regression and classification

### Week 6: Big Data Technologies
- Overview of big data ecosystem
- Tools like Hadoop and Spark

### Week 7: Application of Analytics in Business
- Case studies showcasing the use of analytics in various industries

### Week 8: Project Presentation
- Students present their analytical projects using data-driven insights.

## Assessment
- Assignments: 40%
- Mid-term Exam: 20%
- Final Project: 40%

## Recommended Reading
- "Data Science for Business" by Foster Provost and Tom Fawcett
- "Big Data: A Revolution That Will Transform How We Live, Work, and Think" by Viktor Mayer-Schönberger and Kenneth Cukier
